How to choose the right dog DNA test

A good purchase decision is mostly about clarifying your goal and matching it to the kit’s strengths.

  1. Decide your main goal: breed identification, health screening, or both.
  2. Check what “health” actually includes: marker coverage differs by kit and region.
  3. Look for transparency: confidence scoring, reference breeds, and explanation quality.
  4. Plan for follow-up: health-related flags should be discussed with a veterinarian.
  5. Compare total cost: add-ons, subscriptions, re-tests, and shipping can change value.

Dog DNA test FAQ

Can a dog DNA test diagnose disease?

No. Consumer tests can flag selected genetic markers or risks, but they are not diagnostic medical tests. Treat results as informational and consult a veterinarian for medical decisions.

Do dog DNA results ever change?

They can. Providers may update reference databases and models over time, which can adjust breed estimates or interpretations.

What if my dog is a rescue with unknown background?

That’s one of the most common reasons to test. Breed mix estimates can provide useful context, especially for training and activity planning.
